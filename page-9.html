Why Bloggers Must Understand Tax on Ad Revenue

Whether you’re a high school student running a blog from your bedroom or a teacher sharing educational reels, if you earn money from AdSense, PropellerAds, or any ad network, **that income is taxable under "Income from Other Sources"** in India (and similar categories in the US, UK, Canada, etc.).

The Income Tax Department doesn’t distinguish between "hobby blogging" and "professional blogging" once your earnings cross the basic exemption limit (₹2.5 lakh in India). So even if you’re using MultiPostMaestro to create posts for fun, once you start earning, **you must declare it in your tax return**.

Tax Rules for Student Bloggers in India

Many students think, “I’m under 18, so I don’t need to pay tax.” This is a myth.

If a minor earns income from their own efforts (like blogging), it’s not clubbed with parents’ income (unlike interest or rental income). Instead, the student must file their own return if earnings exceed ₹2.5 lakh.

Example: - Priya, age 16, runs a blog using MultiPostMaestro. - She earns ₹18,000/month from AdSense → ₹2.16 lakh/year. - Since this is below ₹2.5 lakh, no tax is due, but she should still maintain records. - If she crosses ₹2.5 lakh, she must file ITR-4 (for business income) or ITR-1 (if other conditions met).

Do You Need to Pay GST on Blogging Income?

In India, if your total service income exceeds ₹20 lakh/year (₹10 lakh for special states), GST registration is mandatory. Blogging is considered a “service”.

However, if your income is only from AdSense (Google pays you from Singapore), it’s often treated as “export of service” and **GST may not apply**. But consult a CA!

Tax Deductions You Can Claim

As a blogger, you can reduce taxable income by claiming expenses like:

  • Internet bills (partial)
  • Laptop/phone depreciation
  • Website hosting/domain costs

Keep bills and records for 6 years (ITR scrutiny period).
